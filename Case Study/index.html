Building the portfolio of Jack Wangelin

Creating a Brand

Building a brand is a most unusual quandry. The primary issue: selling ones self.

Before arriving at the morale issue, how does one create a brand of ones self? The basics for any company encompass what a personal brand as well.

  • Vision
  • Mission
  • Values
  • Objectives
  • Audiences
  • Positioning
  • Personality

These are the things of a corporate brand. Do people have them as well? When they are trying to get a job they sure do. Creating a brand can be reframed as preparing to present for a job interview, or even just a project.

What is your vision; for your time at the company, or your expectations for the outcomes of the project. Now take that & aggrandize it as though your project is your life. It will sound absurd.

For Jack: his vison:

i want to be a designer because people are lazy idiots. they need a guiding hand to maintain free thought & communication. they are happy to take convenience over conscientiousness, which is problematic in itself: creating communication to make people understand the value of thought through experience is the viaduct of passive subversion. subversion by design. option c. blah blah blah. I don’t need to change the world, i want to change how people think. I don’t have all the answers, but I know where to start.

Oh, it is so rough & gritty.A perfect start. Remember it's not for public consumption.

How does one complete their vision? By starting on their mission. Why do must the vision be brought to fruition? How will it become?

For Jack: his Mission

in every situation, They always present 2 options: buy or don’t buy; pink or blue; on or off; yes or no. I design option c. option c is what you want, the option that fits, the option that the system abhors, but that fills the vacuum.

My Mission. This is the part where I’m supposed to tell you how I am going to change the world, save the whales, or the Chihuahuas or whatever the flavour of the week is. I don’t much care about the world. It has been around for about 4.54 billion years, & will continue to exist after humans have abandoned it. It will be fine.

People. People on the other hand, could use a good talking to; I want to be the one talking. I want to change how people think through design. By taking their brain by the wrinkles & stuffing option c into their minds, by showing the user what they want can be an option. I want to change how people think.

Option C Option c is what you want, the thing that, when you look at the offerings, you think, “why the hell can’t I just have this waffle with fried chicken and ice cream?” It is the option that fits, the option that the system abhors, but that fills the vacuum between dreams & reality.

Now, the values. To direct how the mission may be completed. We can't just go connecting babies to the grid once we find a way to turn cuteness into electricity... Or can we?

For Jack: His Values:

  • live free or die, but in either case, make sure the trip is interesting.
  • free thought, always
  • subvert subversion
  • reason & logic are necessary
  • There’s always an option c
  • don’t get caught.

A bit rough around the edges, but who are we to judge?

Objectives are the most straight forward of the lot. There's no mixed-messages here, objectives are what you think they are: goals.

For Jack: His Objectives:

  • live free or die, but in either case, make sure the trip is interesting.
  • Deliver Option C
  • be candid & concise
  • get other people to realize they can think for themselves
  • communicate without sounding crazy
  • don’t get caught.

Audience for a business or corporation is a much more straight forward ordeal than trying to define the audience for a person. Companies are working to make money. That means that someone has to buy something from them. Figure out who's buying, & you've made your mark.

As a person, the direct question is,

"who wants to buy me?"
What a question: how does one sell ones self & figure out who wants to buy. It's a ridiculous thing, even in the context of being hired, "Whom do I want to work for that wants to hire me?" Bloody difficult if you ask us.

For Jack: his audience:

Is still a work in progress.

Positioning? More like posturing, truth be told. Make a bold statement that you can live up to: your highest level of confidence, the thing that you want to be doing, in the way you want to do it for the rest of your work life.

For Jack: His Position:

"I deliver Option C."

Finally, the personality of a brand. Curiously enough, at the end of the day, this is not something that can really be controlled. You can plan it out, test it out on friends, family & even focus groups, but once it goes live, the internet will have its way with even the bests of brands.

For Jack: His Brand Personality:

  • Get off my damned lawn.
  • When we’re doing business, it’s for the money, so get to the point.
  • When we’re not doing business, it’s none of your business.

These points could definitely use some work, & for the final product, they were modified or tossed out.

User Flows

They can't always be perfect the first time around. The site was designed to direct peopele to the "Option C" section of the philosophy page. During user testing, it was discovered that most people do not scroll down to the lower part of the home page on their first visit, instead, opting to hit the menu button. The final user flow shows how the user is more likely to get to the goal of giving Jack their email address.
